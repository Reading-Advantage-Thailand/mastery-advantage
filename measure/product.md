# Product Definition - Mastery Advantage

## Product Thesis

Mastery Advantage is the company-wide specification and source-data authority
for domain-neutral Knowledge Space Theory plus spaced-repetition systems used
across Advantage learning applications.

It succeeds when application teams can adopt stable, validated graph contracts
and domain datasets without independently redefining mastery, readiness,
provenance, recommendation, or SRS behavior.

## Primary Users

- **Application engineering teams** consume versioned specifications, graph
  artifacts, and integration contracts.
- **Curriculum and language specialists** review source alignment, lexical
  groupings, relationships, and pedagogical usefulness.
- **Data and recommendation teams** calibrate ranking signals and evaluate
  graph quality against learner and content outcomes.
- **Students and teachers** indirectly benefit from accurate mastery tracking,
  review scheduling, next-skill guidance, and reading recommendations.

## Current Product Scope

- Domain-neutral KST+SRS specification and contracts.
- English GSE objective graph shared by Reading Advantage and Primary
  Advantage.
- English CEFR/Cambridge lexical graph for vocabulary SRS and extensive-reading
  article recommendation.
- Domain planning and visual-language assets for the wider Advantage suite.

## Vocabulary Graph Outcome

The lexical graph should enable applications to:

1. Identify lexical skills consistently across content and student evidence.
2. Track receptive and productive vocabulary through SRS.
3. Group vocabulary through source-backed themes and pedagogical units.
4. Rank useful next vocabulary without inventing false prerequisite claims.
5. Recommend articles with high known-word coverage and bounded target-word
   exposure.
6. Explain every important alignment, grouping, relationship, and ranking
   signal through provenance.

## Non-Goals

- Shipping application-specific storage, UI, or recommendation runtime code.
- Treating CEFR bands, book unit numbers, frequency, or embedding similarity as
  hard prerequisite truth.
- Republishing source PDFs.
- Claiming complete English lexical coverage from Cambridge exam lists alone.
